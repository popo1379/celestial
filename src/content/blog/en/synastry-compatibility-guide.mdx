When two people meet, their natal charts don't just sit side by side — they interact. **Synastry** is the branch of astrology that overlays one person's chart onto another's to map out the chemistry, friction, and longevity of a relationship. Here's how to read it.

## 1. The Foundation: Sun, Moon, and Venus

Before diving into aspects, look at three placements in each chart:

- **Sun** — identity and life direction
- **Moon** — emotional needs
- **Venus** — love language and values

Strong compatibility often shows up as flowing aspects (trine, sextile, conjunction) between these three across both charts.

### Classic compatibility pairs

| Aspect | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Person A's Sun trine Person B's Moon | Mutual understanding, emotional warmth |
| Person A's Venus conjunct Person B's Mars | Magnetic attraction, romantic chemistry |
| Person A's Moon conjunct Person B's Moon | Emotional resonance, similar instincts |
| Person A's Sun opposite Person B's Moon | Complementary, classic "opposites attract" pairing |

## 2. Aspects: Harmonious vs. Challenging

Don't mistake challenging aspects for incompatibility. The healthiest relationships usually mix ease and friction.

**Harmonious (flow):**
- Trines (120°) — natural understanding
- Sextiles (60°) — mutual opportunity
- Conjunctions (0°) — merged energy, sometimes too close

**Challenging (tension):**
- Squares (90°) — friction that drives growth
- Oppositions (180°) — polarity, attraction through difference

A chart with only trines can feel flat — no growth. A chart with only squares can feel exhausting. A mix is ideal.

## 3. House Overlays

When one person's planets fall into the houses of the other person's chart, they light up that life area:

- **Partner's Sun in your 7th house** — they feel like "the one," the archetypal partner
- **Partner's Moon in your 4th house** — they feel like home, family
- **Partner's Venus in your 5th house** — romance feels playful and creative
- **Partner's Saturn in your 10th house** — they support (or pressure) your career

House overlays describe **where** each person activates the other's life.

## 4. The Composite Chart

Beyond synastry, astrologers also build a **composite chart** — a single chart that represents the relationship itself, using the midpoint of each pair of planets.

Key composite placements:

- **Composite Sun** — the relationship's identity and purpose
- **Composite Moon** — the relationship's emotional climate
- **Composite Venus** — how the relationship expresses love
- **Composite Saturn** — the relationship's longevity and structure

A strong composite Saturn (well-aspected) often signals staying power.

## 5. Red Flags and Green Flags

**Green flags:**
- Sun-Moon, Moon-Venus harmony
- Mutual Saturn support (grounding)
- Jupiter contacts (growth and laughter together)
- Mutual Ascendant/Descendant contacts

**Yellow flags (workable, but work):**
- Heavy squares from Saturn to inner planets
- Neptune-Venus hard aspects (idealization risk)
- Uranus on the Moon (instability in emotional life)

**Red flags:**
- Repeated harsh Pluto-to-personal-planet contacts without harmonious counterweights
- Saturn squaring the other's Sun, Moon, or Venus (often coldness or control)

## 6. Compatibility Is a Conversation, Not a Score

Two charts don't have to be perfect for a relationship to thrive. Synastry is most useful for understanding *how* two people interact, not for predicting success or failure. A "60% compatibility" chart can become a lifelong partnership if both people are willing to grow; a "95%" chart can fizzle if no one does the work.
