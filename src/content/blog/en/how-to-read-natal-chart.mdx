A natal chart (also called a birth chart) is a map of where the Sun, Moon, and planets were at the exact moment you were born, viewed from your birthplace. If you've ever wondered why astrology feels so personally accurate, it's because no two natal charts are exactly alike. This guide walks you through every layer of a natal chart so you can read yours with confidence.

## 1. The Big Three: Sun, Moon, and Rising

The fastest way to understand any chart is to start with the **Big Three**.

- **Sun sign** — your core identity, ego, and life purpose. This is the "zodiac sign" most people know.
- **Moon sign** — your emotional world, instincts, and inner needs.
- **Rising sign (Ascendant)** — the mask you wear, your outward personality, and how others first perceive you.

Your rising sign also determines the starting point of your 12 houses, so it sets the entire structure of your chart.

## 2. The 10 Planets

Modern Western astrology uses 10 celestial bodies:

1. **Sun** — ego, vitality
2. **Moon** — emotions, habits
3. **Mercury** — communication, thinking
4. **Venus** — love, values, pleasure
5. **Mars** — action, desire, drive
6. **Jupiter** — growth, belief, luck
7. **Saturn** — structure, responsibility, limits
8. **Uranus** — change, originality
9. **Neptune** — dreams, spirituality
10. **Pluto** — transformation, power

Each planet sits in a zodiac sign (the *how*) and a house (the *where* of your life).

## 3. The 12 Zodiac Signs

The signs describe the *style* or *flavor* a planet takes on:

| Element | Signs |
|--------|-------|
| Fire | Aries, Leo, Sagittarius |
| Earth | Taurus, Virgo, Capricorn |
| Air | Gemini, Libra, Aquarius |
| Water | Cancer, Scorpio, Pisces |

Fire signs are bold and inspired, Earth signs are practical and grounded, Air signs are mental and social, and Water signs are emotional and intuitive.

## 4. The 12 Houses

Houses represent the **life areas** where planetary energy plays out. The Whole Sign house system — which we use at Celestial — assigns one zodiac sign to each house, starting from your rising sign:

- **1st house:** Self, identity, body
- **2nd house:** Money, possessions, values
- **3rd house:** Communication, siblings, short trips
- **4th house:** Home, family, roots
- **5th house:** Creativity, romance, children
- **6th house:** Work, health, daily routines
- **7th house:** Partnerships, marriage
- **8th house:** Transformation, shared resources
- **9th house:** Travel, higher education, beliefs
- **10th house:** Career, public reputation
- **11th house:** Friends, community, dreams
- **12th house:** Spirituality, the unconscious, solitude

## 5. The Major Aspects

Aspects are angles between planets that describe how they interact:

- **Conjunction (0°):** energies merge
- **Sextile (60°):** flowing opportunity
- **Square (90°):** internal tension and growth
- **Trine (120°):** harmony and ease
- **Opposition (180°):** polarity and balance

Squares and oppositions are not "bad" — they are where life asks you to grow.

## 6. Putting It All Together

To interpret any placement, combine three layers:

> Planet (what) + Sign (how) + House (where)

For example, **Venus in Scorpio in the 7th house** means: your love nature (Venus) is intense, private, and loyal (Scorpio), playing out primarily in partnerships and marriage (7th house).
